\fbox{\parbox{\dimexpr\linewidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{
12.4.2
Suppose an electron is in the spin state
$|s\rangle=(1/\sqrt2)(|{\uparrow}\rangle+i|{\uparrow}\rangle)$,
and the electron spin magnetic moment operator can be written as
$\hat{\boldsymbol\mu}_e=g\mu_B\hat{\boldsymbol\sigma}$.
What is the expectation value of the electron spin magnetic moment?
}}

\bigskip
For
\begin{equation*}
|s\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1+i\\0\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

we have
\begin{align*}
\langle s|\hat\sigma_x|s\rangle&=0
\\
\langle s|\hat\sigma_y|s\rangle&=0
\\
\langle s|\hat\sigma_z|s\rangle&=1
\end{align*}

Hence the expectation value is
\begin{equation*}
\langle\hat{\boldsymbol\mu}_e\rangle
=g\mu_B\langle s|\hat{\boldsymbol\sigma}|s\rangle
=g\mu_B\begin{pmatrix}0\\0\\1\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}
