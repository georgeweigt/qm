\FBOX{
10-5. Consider a spin-1/2 system represented by the normalized spinor
\begin{equation*}
\chi=\begin{pmatrix}\cos\alpha\\\sin\alpha\exp(i\beta)\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

in the Pauli representation, where $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are real.
What is the probability that a measurement of $S_y$ yields $-\hbar/2$?
}

The spin-1/2 eigenstates are
\begin{align*}
|x_+\rangle&=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}(1,1) &
|y_+\rangle&=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}(1,i) &
|z_+\rangle&=(1,0)
\\
|x_-\rangle&=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}(1,-1) &
|y_-\rangle&=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}(1,-i) &
|z_-\rangle&=(0,1)
\end{align*}

Hence
\begin{equation*}
\Pr\left(S_y=-\tfrac{\hbar}{2}\right)
=|\langle y_-|\chi\rangle|^2
=\tfrac{1}{2}-\tfrac{1}{2}\sin(2\alpha)\sin\beta
\end{equation*}
