\fbox{\parbox{\dimexpr\linewidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{
24-5.
Let $L_1$, $L_2$, $L_3$ be the matrices that represent
orbital angular momentum, and let
\begin{equation*}
{J_1}'=-\frac{1}{\hbar}L_2,\quad
{J_2}'=\frac{1}{\hbar}L_1,\quad
{J_3}'=\frac{1}{\hbar}L_3.
\end{equation*}

Show that ${J_1}'$, ${J_2}'$, ${J_3}'$ satisfy the commutation
relations characteristic of matrices $J_1$, $J_2$, $J_3$
that correspond to rotations, but with the
matrices $Q_1$, $Q_2$, $Q_3$ and $P_1$, $P_2$, $P_3$ that represent
position and momentum, ${J_1}'$, ${J_2}'$, ${J_3}'$ do
not satisfy the commutation relations that
characterize rotations of vector quantities.
}}

\bigskip
We have
\begin{align*}
{J_1}'{J_2}'-{J_2}'{J_1}'&=-\frac{1}{\hbar^2}L_2L_1+\frac{1}{\hbar^2}L_1L_2
\\
&=\frac{1}{\hbar^2}(L_1L_2-L_2L_1)
\\
&=\frac{1}{\hbar^2}(i\hbar L_3)
\\
&=i{J_3}'
\end{align*}

and similarly for the remaining commutation relations.

\bigskip
For the second part of the problem the requirement for a rotation is
\begin{align*}
Q_1{J_2}'-{J_2}'Q_1&=iQ_3
\\
Q_2{J_3}'-{J_3}'Q_2&=iQ_1
\\
Q_3{J_1}'-{J_1}'Q_3&=iQ_2
\end{align*}

However what we get is
\begin{align*}
Q_1{J_2}'-{J_2}'Q_1&=0
\\
Q_2{J_3}'-{J_3}'Q_2&=iQ_1
\\
Q_3{J_1}'-{J_1}'Q_3&=iQ_1
\end{align*}

and similarly with $P_1$, $P_2$, and $P_3$.
