\fbox{\parbox{\dimexpr\linewidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{
24-1.
Show that multiplying the matrix
$1-i\varepsilon M_3-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon^2M_3^2$
by the similar matrix with $\varepsilon$ replaced by $\varepsilon'$
gives another matrix of this form where $\varepsilon$ is replaced by
$\varepsilon+\varepsilon'$, in the approximation where terms quadratic
in $\varepsilon$ and $\varepsilon'$ are included but terms with higher
powers are ignored.
This shows that the product of
two rotations measured by $\varepsilon$ and $\varepsilon'$
is the rotation measured by $\varepsilon+\varepsilon'$.
In the particular case where $\varepsilon'$ is $-\varepsilon$,
the product is the identity rotation,
so the second rotation is the inverse of the first.
}}

\bigskip
In matrix form
\begin{multline*}
\begin{pmatrix}1-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon^2&-\varepsilon&0\\
\varepsilon&1-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon^2&0\\
0&0&1\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}1-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon'^2&-\varepsilon'&0\\
\varepsilon'&1-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon'^2&0\\
0&0&1\end{pmatrix}
\\
{}\approx\begin{pmatrix}
1-\varepsilon\varepsilon'-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon^2-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon'^2&-\varepsilon-\varepsilon'&0\\
\varepsilon+\varepsilon'&1-\varepsilon\varepsilon'-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon^2-\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon'^2&0\\
0&0&1\end{pmatrix}
\end{multline*}
