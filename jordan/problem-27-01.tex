Starting with the equations for $R_1$, $R_2$, $R_3$ in this

$Q_1$, $Q_2$, $Q_3$; and $P_1$, $P_2$, $P_3$ given in Chapter 20,
find the formulas for $Q_{e1}$, $Q_{e2}$, $Q_{e3}$; $P_{e1}$, $P_{e2}$,
$P_{e3}$ and $Q_{n1}$, $Q_{n2}$, $Q_{n3}$; $P_{n1}$, $P_{n2}$, $P_{n3}$
in terms of $R_1$, $R_2$, $R_3$; $\hbar K_1$, $\hbar K_2$, $\hbar K_3$

\bigskip
For the 1 direction we have
\begin{align*}
R_1&=\frac{m_e}{m_n+m_e}Q_{e1}+\frac{m_n}{m_n+m_e}Q_{n1}
\\
Q_1&=Q_{e1}-Q_{n1}
\end{align*}

and
\begin{align*}
P_1&=\frac{m_n}{m_n+m_e}P_{e1}-\frac{m_e}{m_n+m_e}P_{n1}
\\
\hbar K_1&=P_{e1}+P_{n1}
\end{align*}

Substitute $Q_1$ into $R_1$ to obtain
\begin{align*}
Q_{e1}&=R_1+\frac{m_n}{m_n+m_e}Q_1
\\
Q_{n1}&=R_1-\frac{m_e}{m_n+m_e}Q_1
\end{align*}

Substitute $\hbar K_1$ into $P_1$ to obtain
\begin{align*}
P_{e1}&=\frac{m_e}{m_n+m_e}\hbar K_1+P_1
\\
P_{n1}&=\frac{m_n}{m_n+m_e}\hbar K_1-P_1
\end{align*}
