\fbox{\parbox{\dimexpr\linewidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{
Exercise 1.1.
(a) Using the axioms for inner products, prove
\begin{equation*}
\{\langle A|+\langle B|\}|C\rangle=\langle A|C\rangle+\langle B|C\rangle
\end{equation*}

(b) Prove $\langle A|A\rangle$ is a real number.
}}

\bigskip
(a) Trivial by linearity.

\bigskip
(b) By the second axiom
\begin{equation*}
\langle A|A\rangle=\langle A|A\rangle^*
\end{equation*}

Hence the imaginary part of $\langle A|A\rangle$ is zero.
